# Qwen3-Embedding at Native 4096d

**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Dataset:** LoCoMo conv-0, 199 questions
**Config:** Dense-only, qwen3-embedding:8b at native 4096d via Sunbreaker

## Hypothesis

Qwen3-embedding at 768d matched nomic due to Matryoshka truncation. At native
4096d, the richer embedding space should improve retrieval metrics.

## Setup

- Qdrant collection created at 4096d
- Embedding via Sunbreaker (192.168.1.69:11434) — qwen3-embedding:8b
- No truncation — full 4096d vectors stored and searched
- All pipeline stages disabled (dense-only)

## Results

| Metric | Baseline (nomic 768d) | Qwen3 4096d | Delta |
|--------|----------------------|-------------|-------|
| Non-adv accuracy | 65.1% | 59.9% | -5.2pp |
| Overall accuracy | 51.3% | 45.7% | -5.6pp |

Per-category:
| Category | Baseline | Qwen3 4096d | Delta |
|----------|----------|-------------|-------|
| single-hop | 46.9% | 40.6% | -6.3pp |
| multi-hop | 61.5% | 46.2% | -15.3pp |
| temporal | 59.5% | 59.5% | 0pp |
| open-domain | 77.1% | 71.4% | -5.7pp |
| adversarial | 6.4% | 8.5% | +2.1pp |

Retrieval oracle (open-domain retrieval miss): 18% → 55%. Worse retrieval.

## Verdict: REVERT

4096d qwen3-embedding is WORSE than nomic-embed-text at 768d for conversational
memory. The richer embedding space doesn't help — the model may not be well-suited
for short, informal conversational text. The embedding model is definitively not
the bottleneck for this benchmark.
